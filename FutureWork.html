Results

Future Work

The most pressing next step for this project is to further investigate the overfitting in the NMF model. Shuffling randomly from songs above the threshold alleviates this issue but the raw modeI appears to be favouring predictions from existing dense playlists rather than scoring highly from a span of similar playlists. To improve this moving forward we’d like to implement a form of penalty or regularization for the model or rethink the scoring metric altogether. Interestingly, even though we see evidence of overfitting when viewing the results manually, the model performs the best on the held out test set indicating that the scoring methodology is relatively rudimentary and could also use further improvement.

One method which could improve the NMF model further is to focus more on the hyperparameter tuning aspects. As discussed in the modelling segment, there are a number of competing hyperparameters which could be tuned and cross-validated to potentially improve the performance of the model. In this report however we were limited by our ability to create an accurate scoring function and hence relied on cosine-similarity between ‘n’ playlists. Given more time we would like to further investigate our optimization approach of the hyperparameters, especially:

  • Selection of a more robust similarity or loss metric.
  • Further penalty terms in the scoring function to further constrain the problem and rely on fewer heuristic values.
An obvious and easily attainable extension with some data engineering effort would be to fit and test the model on more of the overall million playlist data. During this report a random subsample was used as a proof of concept. Whilst training on more data shouldn’t necessarily provide an improvement in the model performance it would make it more usable moving forward as the more songs the model has access to, the more it can recommend from.

Finally we’d also like to perform further feature engineering to bolster our understanding of the user - song relationship. Explicit information such as user reviews or ratings of songs would drastically improve our ability to expand our collaborative filtering systems. We could also include song lyrics or further metadata available through the Spotify API. Throughout this report we also treated all playlists as the same weighting, giving them equal credit. What would be an interesting extension would be to include a weighting based on playlist popularity (such as number of followers or likes) which may implicitly indicate user preferences.
