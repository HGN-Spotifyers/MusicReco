EDA

Our EDA

We were initially provided with Spotify’s million playlist dataset, which was spread across a selection of 1000 csv files. In order to best represent this information, we wanted to turn this into a single binary occurrence matrix with unique songs as rows, and playlists as columns, with minimal memory impact

We used track_uri as a unique identifier to ensure there would be no duplicates, and stored respective records for album, artist and song’s relative playlist position with the track name we leveraged Google Colab to read in the raw data from each file and read line by line, adding unseen songs to our dataframe and updating occurrences of existing songs on the new column for the playlist in question. We further exploited use of IO (writing to disk) and reading the csv’s iteratively to save it in a file of a reasonable size

General Stats

Overall, we found 2,262,292 unique tracks, and each song appears on an average of 29 playlists. The million playlists range in length from 3 to 341 tracks, and have an average length is 65 tracks.

We observed that the majority of songs are on only 1 or 2 playlists (median=2), whereas a relatively small number of songs appear on a huge number of playlists:


Upon querying the Spotify API, we were fortunate that all songs present had non-null metric values, meaning that we were not required to make any data imputations.

Upon aggregating general song metrics, we wanted to understand the distribution of metrics values -- both for the range of values that they can assume and also to understand if any of the distributions were heavily skewed (a point to keep in mind when making model assumptions).
  • Danceability, valence and tempo were the only reasonably symmetric plots. This is reassuring that there is a more even spread of these values that we are exposed to in the data. These may show themselves to be strong values to split on.
  • Speechiness, acousticness, instrumentalness and liveness are all heavily right skewed. This may indicate that it will be harder to separate or uniquely identify songs based on these characteristics if they are all so low.
  • On the other hand, mode is bimodal and key has equal distribution around each integer. These may be weaker predictors on their own.

We further wanted to understand any correlations between behaviors of these individual song metric variables. Using a sample of 170k songs, we understood a few major correlations:
  • Loudness and energy have a strong negative correlation with acousticness
  • Key appears to be relatively independent of other types of metric
  • Songs that have high instrumentalness also tend to have lower loudness, whereas high energy corresponds to more loudness.
  • It also made sense to see that danceability was positively correlated with valence which is indirectly a measure of "happiness" in a song.

Song Characteristics PCA

A very important step in our approach was to perform PCA on the song/playlist space and on the song/characteristics space. This dimensionality reduction is key in distilling features and similarities between songs, however it is often hard to interpret or visualize how it is working. For our EDA we wanted to understand how these two spaces are decomposed using PCA before moving into other techniques like Matrix factorization. Viewing both the songs characteristics and shared playlist space will give us an understanding of what information we can capture and whether there is a true signal to be used for modelling.

We quickly realized that focusing on individual song metrics such as tempo or valence does not lend itself well to distinct separation between two songs: for example both rap and country can have high speechiness metrics. This is why we decided to perform PCA to distill down the multidimensional song metrics.

Representing the combined song characteristics in fewer components would allow us to more easily score song closeness/similarity. Below is a breakdown of the first two principal components of the song characteristics, colored by the different characteristics themselves:


We can see that the principal components of the metrics space are able to clearly divide many of the song metrics by themselves. We can clearly see a divide (in the first two PC space) between different "types" of song - acousticness is high towards the far right and low toward the top left which is almost exactly opposite to energy (which would make sense from a musical theory perspective).

Beyond the first two PCs, we can see that between the 3rd and 4th PCs, we are able to capture further metric separations - between “key” - that weren’t separable between the first two. This shows how the PCA is effectively "encoding" different elements on the songs across the different principal component dimensions.

Playlist Space PCA

We followed this investigation with an exploration of PCA dimensionality reduction of the “playlist space” -- representing relationships between songs and their presence on playlists. By compressing the large matrix of songs vs playlists into fewer dimensions, we should be able to detect regions of similar songs based on their proximity. To verify this hypothesis, we highlighted a region of songs belonging to a particular rap playlist on the 1x2 PC space. We can see a general clustering of these songs here. What is more interesting is that if we instead overlay the variable of speechiness on the plot, we can also see a general clustering and region formed by these songs which actually overlaps with the rap playlist mentioned before, and aligns with general characteristics of the rap genre.


This confirms by doing playlist-based PCA, we are inherently incorporating some underlying information and trends which are based on the raw metrics of these songs in addition to more qualitative “human touch” measures like mood, genre or theme. This would be a good starting point for our modeling.
