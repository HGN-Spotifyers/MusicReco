Models

KNN Model on PCA Space of Song/Playlist Membership
In order to capture nuances of playlist composition, we must incorporate information about song’s playlist membership. It is clear that what belongs on a playlist may not be as simple as adding on more songs with the mean valence/tempo/etc of the songs on the playlist.

The first of the collaborative filtering models is the PCA-KNN, which operates on the song-playlist space. This model captures inherent user preferences by reducing the song to playlist matrix down from all playlists to a subset of principal (core) components which best describe the variance of the original space -- compressing the information of all the different playlists particular songs were and were not members of.

From the EDA we discovered that this space appears to sensibly divide both song characteristics and known genre based playlists into different areas of space, indicating its reasonable ability to “understand” and incorporate these measures.

Above, we can see above how a given country playlist occupies a certain region in this PCA space. The characteristics of songs in this region also tend to have high instrumentalness and generally high acousticness. As this PCA space is able to encode and separate these multiple metrics, the hope is that this KNN model will perform well on finding similar or new songs for playlists.

Our method for this PCA space model was similar to that of the previous baseline song characteristics-based KNN model. The space was created using the first 12 principal components, and the 10 closest neighbors were identified for each song on a given playlist. The pool of recommended songs is then generated, again, removing songs that already exist on the playlists and overlapping songs that appear multiple times as neighbors are weighted higher. Recommendations are thus made in descending order of occurrence and then randomly.

We will investigate the performance of this playlist PCA space KNN against the song attributes PCA space KNN in the results section.
