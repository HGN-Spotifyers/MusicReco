Model Comparisons¶

The table below contains the resulting scores for each model predicting on the randomly selected test playlists with each of the three different metrics. It is clear that classification accuracy is not a reliable metric for scoring, the values are far too low and hence slight random perturbations (predicting one more exactly correct song) could have too great an effect on the score. Both distance to the held out songâ€™s centroid and distance to the playlist centroid provide comparable scores.

We can see from the histograms above how the two distance metrics varied for the 3 different models. We can see that the metrics pca model (in blue) tended to have the least right skewed spread with more distances at higher values when evaluated with the holdout set metric. This model is followed by the playlist PCA model and then by the NMF model, in terms of highest frequencies at low distances.

However, the distributions were different when scoring was calculated on the whole playlist. Here, the PCA model on playlist space had a higher frequency of low distances, followed then by nmf and attributes based PCA.

From these graphs we can see that the NMF model is performing better at predicting more songs which are very close to the centre of the held out songs, indicating that it is performing in the most human-esque manner out of the three models.

For future testing, it may be interesting to incorporate a measure of variance of the prediction cluster. We may not be able to capture enough information by just optimizing for the average characteristics of either the left out songs or the playlist as a whole. A variance measure would also ensure that the distribution of songs in the prediction more closely matches the variety of those withheld songs or the playlist as whole.

Taking the top predictions of each model on a random selection of Christmas songs (not a pre-existing playlist but 10 songs chosen at random containing the word Christmas) it becomes very clear that the baseline model does not suggest relevant predictions. The songs returned appear to be an eclectic mix with no discernable thread which connects them, presumably however they will be nearby in attribute space. Both the PCA and NMF models clearly find the relevance of Christmas to the original playlist and recommend a selection of only Christmas songs, which is a positive result in both these modelâ€™s favor.

Manually inspecting the returned playlists highlights a very interesting result between the NMF model and the PCA model. The songs returned from both the PCA and NMF model appear very similar on the surface, they are both clearly relevant Christmas songs. The key difference however is that the NMF songs all appear to be coming from relatively fewer playlists than the songs from the PCA model. Whilst not inherently a bad thing, and resulting in it outperforming PCA on the test scores, it does indicate that the NMF model is overfit to specific playlists within the dataset and prefers to draw many songs from only a few core playlists. Empirically these appear to be the larger playlists and so we may wish to downweight the effects of these unusually large playlists in the future.
